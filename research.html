Research Interests

I have a broad interest in aquatic biology, biodiversity and their evolution. Based in Southern China, our lab has a strong focus on Indo-West Pacific fauna that are highly diverse - ecologically and phylogenetically - while also increasing threatened due to global climate change and local development. We strive to unveil the fascinating biology, evolution, and adaptive features of diverse fauna in this marine and freshwater biodiversity hotspot. Ongoing projects includes:

Crustacean phylogenomics and phylophysiology

We develop genome-scale markers for sequence capture (AHE, UCE, exons) and PCR-based NPCL sequencing to scrutinize the phylogenetic relationships of crustacean lineages. These robust phylogenies will be frameworks for examining how diverse physiological and adaptive features evolved.

Historical Biogeography of South China Aquatic Fauna

Freshwater shrimps in family Atyidae have one of the most species rich group of crustacean with diverse life styles, sizes, habitats, and colour patterns. Through extensive surveys, and cutting-edge phylogenomic and biogeographic analyses, and genome surveys we strive to understand how this diverse freshwater fauna evolved.
